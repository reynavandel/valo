1. What is the aim of Experiment 5?

Answer:
To use word embeddings to generate meaningful sentences and create a short paragraph from a given seed word.

2. What is a seed word?

Answer:
A seed word is the initial input word provided by the user to generate similar words and create a paragraph.

Example: ocean, adventure, technology

3. Which word embedding model is used?

Answer:
GloVe (glove-wiki-gigaword-100)

4. Why is GloVe used?

Answer:
GloVe is used to find semantically similar words for the given seed word.

5. What does most_similar() do?

Answer:
It retrieves the top-N semantically similar words for the given word.

6. What is the purpose of get_similar_words()?

Answer:
It retrieves the top 5 similar words from the GloVe model.

7. What is the purpose of generate_sentence()?

Answer:
It creates a meaningful sentence using the seed word and the retrieved similar words.

8. What is the purpose of generate_paragraph()?

Answer:
It combines multiple generated sentences into a short creative paragraph.

9. Why is random.choice() used?

Answer:
It randomly selects one sentence template, making the generated paragraph different each time.

10. What happens if the seed word is not found?

Answer:
The program displays a message asking the user to try another word.

11. What is the role of sentence templates?

Answer:
Sentence templates help generate grammatically meaningful sentences using the similar words.

12. What is word embedding?

Answer:
Word embedding is a numerical vector representation of words that captures semantic meaning and relationships.

13. What are the applications of this experiment?

Answer:

Story generation
Creative writing
Content creation
Idea generation
Text generation
14. Why do we use a pre-trained model instead of training one?

Answer:
A pre-trained model is already trained on a large dataset, providing accurate semantic relationships without additional training.

15. Why are similar words important?

Answer:
They help generate richer and more meaningful sentences by providing related vocabulary.

16. Explain the flow of Experiment 5.

Answer:

Import required libraries.
Load the pre-trained GloVe model.
Accept a seed word from the user.
Retrieve the top 5 similar words.
Generate sentences using templates.
Combine the sentences into a paragraph.
Display the generated paragraph.
17. What is creative text generation?

Answer:
Creative text generation is the automatic generation of meaningful and readable text using AI techniques.

18. Can different seed words produce different stories?

Answer:
Yes. Different seed words generate different similar words, resulting in different paragraphs.

19. What is the advantage of using word embeddings for creative writing?

Answer:
Word embeddings help generate contextually related words, making the generated text more meaningful and natural.

20. How is Experiment 5 different from Experiment 4?

Answer:

Experiment 4 improves a prompt and uses GPT-2 to generate text.
Experiment 5 uses GloVe word embeddings and sentence templates to generate a creative paragraph without a Generative AI model.
