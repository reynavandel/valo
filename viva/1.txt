1. What is Word Embedding?

Answer:
Word embedding is a numerical vector representation of words that captures their meaning and semantic relationships.

2. What is Gensim?

Answer:
Gensim is a Python library used for Natural Language Processing (NLP) to work with word embedding models like Word2Vec, GloVe, and FastText.

3. What is GloVe?

Answer:
GloVe (Global Vectors for Word Representation) is a pre-trained word embedding model trained on Wikipedia and Gigaword datasets.

4. Why do we use a pre-trained model?

Answer:
A pre-trained model is already trained on a large dataset, so we don't need to train it again. It saves time and provides accurate word embeddings.

5. Which pre-trained model is used in this experiment?

Answer:
The glove-wiki-gigaword-100 model is used.

6. What does "100" mean in glove-wiki-gigaword-100?

Answer:
It means every word is represented using a 100-dimensional vector.

7. What does api.load() do?

Answer:
It downloads and loads the pre-trained GloVe model into memory.

8. What is Word Arithmetic?

Answer:
Word arithmetic performs mathematical operations on word vectors to discover semantic relationships.

Example:
King - Man + Woman = Queen

9. Why does King - Man + Woman = Queen work?

Answer:
Because word embeddings capture semantic relationships between words, allowing vector arithmetic to represent analogies.

10. What is Cosine Similarity?

Answer:
Cosine similarity measures how similar two word vectors are. A value closer to 1 means the words are more similar.

11. What does wv.similarity() do?

Answer:
It calculates the cosine similarity between two words.

12. What does wv.most_similar() do?

Answer:
It returns the top-N words that are most semantically similar to the given word.

13. Why are "cat" and "dog" highly similar?

Answer:
Because they often appear in similar contexts and have similar meanings.

14. What is a word vector?

Answer:
A word vector is the numerical representation of a word in multidimensional space.

15. What are the applications of Word Embeddings?

Answer:

Machine Translation
Sentiment Analysis
Chatbots
Text Classification
Question Answering
Recommendation Systems
16. What is NLP?

Answer:
Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a branch of Artificial Intelligence that enables computers to understand, process, and generate human language.

17. What is the output of most_similar("happy")?

Answer:
It returns the top similar words like joyful, glad, cheerful, pleased, etc.

18. Can we use other pre-trained models instead of GloVe?

Answer:
Yes. Examples include:

Word2Vec
FastText
BERT
ELMo
19. What is the advantage of Word Embeddings over One-Hot Encoding?

Answer:
Word embeddings capture semantic meaning and relationships, whereas one-hot encoding only represents words as sparse vectors without meaning.

20. Explain the flow of the program.

Answer:

Import Gensim.
Load the pre-trained GloVe model.
Perform word arithmetic.
Calculate similarity between words.
Find similar words using most_similar().
Display the results.
