1. What is the aim of Experiment 3?

Answer:
To train a custom Word2Vec model on a medical corpus, visualize the learned word embeddings, and find semantically similar words.

2. What is Word2Vec?

Answer:
Word2Vec is a neural network-based algorithm that learns vector representations (embeddings) of words from a text corpus.

3. What is a corpus?

Answer:
A corpus is a collection of text or sentences used to train an NLP model.

4. Which domain is used in this experiment?

Answer:
Medical domain.

5. Why is a medical corpus used?

Answer:
To learn domain-specific relationships between medical terms like treatment, diagnosis, vaccine, and therapy.

6. What is tokenization?

Answer:
Tokenization is the process of splitting sentences into individual words (tokens).

7. What does this line do?
processed_corpus = [sentence.lower().split() for sentence in medical_corpus]

Answer:
It converts all sentences to lowercase and splits them into individual words for training.

8. What is the difference between Experiment 1 and Experiment 3?

Answer:

Experiment 1 uses a pre-trained GloVe model.
Experiment 3 trains a custom Word2Vec model on our own dataset.
9. What does vector_size=100 mean?

Answer:
Each word is represented by a vector containing 100 numerical values.

10. What does window=5 mean?

Answer:
The model considers up to 5 neighboring words on either side as context while learning word relationships.

11. What does min_count=1 mean?

Answer:
Every word is included in the vocabulary, even if it appears only once.

12. What does epochs=50 mean?

Answer:
The model is trained on the dataset 50 times to improve learning.

13. Why is workers=4 used?

Answer:
It uses 4 CPU threads to speed up the training process.

14. Why is PCA (or t-SNE in the manual) used?

Answer:
To reduce high-dimensional word vectors into 2 dimensions so they can be visualized on a graph.

15. What does model.wv.most_similar() do?

Answer:
It returns the top-N words that are most semantically similar to the given word.

16. Why do related words appear close together in the graph?

Answer:
Because Word2Vec learns similar vector representations for words that appear in similar contexts.

17. What is a word embedding?

Answer:
A word embedding is a numerical vector representation of a word that captures its meaning and relationships with other words.

18. What are the applications of Word2Vec?

Answer:

Chatbots
Search engines
Text classification
Recommendation systems
Sentiment analysis
Machine translation
19. Explain the flow of Experiment 3.

Answer:

Import required libraries.
Create a medical corpus.
Preprocess the corpus (lowercase and tokenize).
Train the Word2Vec model.
Generate word embeddings.
Reduce dimensions using PCA/t-SNE.
Visualize the embeddings.
Find similar words.
20. Why do we train a custom Word2Vec model?

Answer:
To learn domain-specific semantic relationships from our own dataset instead of relying on a general pre-trained model.
