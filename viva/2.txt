1. What is the aim of Experiment 2?

Answer:
To visualize word embeddings using PCA and perform operations like word arithmetic (Exp 2A) or finding similar words (Exp 2B).

2. What is PCA?

Answer:
PCA (Principal Component Analysis) is a dimensionality reduction technique that converts high-dimensional data into lower dimensions while preserving most of the important information.

3. Why is PCA used in this experiment?

Answer:
Word embeddings are 100-dimensional vectors. PCA reduces them to 2 dimensions so they can be visualized on a graph.

4. What library is used for PCA?

Answer:
sklearn.decomposition.PCA

5. What is the purpose of matplotlib?

Answer:
It is used to plot and visualize the word embeddings on a graph.

6. What does plt.scatter() do?

Answer:
It plots the words as points on a 2D graph.

7. Why do we use plt.text()?

Answer:
It displays the corresponding word beside each plotted point.

8. Why do similar words appear close together in the graph?

Answer:
Because similar words have similar vector representations, so PCA places them near each other.

9. What is the purpose of the visualize() function?

Answer:
It converts word vectors into 2D using PCA and displays them on a scatter plot.

10. What is the input to the visualize() function?

Answer:
A list of words whose embeddings need to be visualized.

11. What does wv[w] return?

Answer:
It returns the word embedding (vector) of the word w.

12. What is the difference between Experiment 2A and Experiment 2B?

Answer:

Experiment 2A performs word arithmetic and visualization.
Experiment 2B finds top similar words and visualization.
13. What is word arithmetic?

Answer:
It performs mathematical operations on word vectors to discover semantic relationships.

Example:
King − Man + Woman = Queen

14. What does most_similar() do in Experiment 2B?

Answer:
It returns the top 5 words that are semantically similar to the given word.

15. Which pre-trained model is used?

Answer:
glove-wiki-gigaword-100

16. What does topn=5 mean?

Answer:
It returns the top 5 most similar words.

17. Why do we use pre-trained embeddings instead of training a model?

Answer:
Pre-trained embeddings are already trained on large datasets, making them accurate and saving training time.

18. What are word embeddings?

Answer:
Word embeddings are numerical vector representations of words that capture semantic meaning and relationships.

19. What are some applications of PCA?

Answer:

Data visualization
Dimensionality reduction
Noise reduction
Machine Learning preprocessing
20. Explain the flow of Experiment 2.

Answer:

Import required libraries.
Load the pre-trained GloVe model.
Extract word vectors.
Apply PCA to reduce dimensions from 100D to 2D.
Plot the graph.
Perform word arithmetic (Exp 2A) or retrieve similar words (Exp 2B).
